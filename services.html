Copywriting

Words that make people buy.

I write captions, newsletters and product descriptions that don't just describe. They make your audience feel something, and then act on it.

Example 1: Skincare brand, overnight face oil

What they currently post

"Our overnight face oil is formulated with rosehip, vitamin E and jojoba. Clinically tested. Wake up to softer, more radiant skin. Shop now."

What you get instead

"Put it on at 11pm. Forget about it. Wake up at 7 and catch your own reflection before you're fully awake. That's the moment we made it for."

The ingredients list tells you nothing you'll remember. The moment you recognise does. Readers buy when they see themselves in the copy.

Example 2: Restaurant, Mediterranean dining

What they currently post

"Join us for modern Mediterranean cuisine using locally sourced, seasonal ingredients. Private dining available. Reserve your table online."

What you get instead

"The kind of meal where you stay two hours longer than you planned and don't mind at all. Mediterranean, locally sourced, embarrassingly good. Book a table."

"Embarrassingly good" is the kind of honest, casual line that stops people mid-scroll. It sounds like a recommendation from a friend, not a brand.
